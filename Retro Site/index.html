Operating Instructions

  1. Wind the hand-crank mechanism clockwise until resistance is felt (approximately 15 turns)
  2. Place the disc record upon the turntable
  3. Gently lower the reproducer needle onto the outer groove of the disc
  4. The apparatus shall commence playing automatically
  5. When the recording concludes, lift the reproducer and remove the disc

The Edison Edison-Bell Company and Our History

Below is a brief overview of the company's rich heritage and contributions to the world of sound recording.

The Edison-Bell Company, established in the late 19th century, was a pioneering force in the realm of sound recording and reproduction. Founded by a consortium of visionary entrepreneurs, the company sought to bring the marvels of Thomas Edison's phonograph to the masses.

Throughout its illustrious history, Edison-Bell has been at the forefront of technological innovation, introducing groundbreaking advancements in recording media, playback mechanisms, and acoustic engineering. The company's commitment to excellence has earned it a distinguished reputation among audiophiles and music enthusiasts alike.

Today, the Edison-Bell Talking Machine Emporium continues this proud legacy, offering a curated selection of the finest gramophones and records available. Our dedication to quality craftsmanship and customer satisfaction remains unwavering as we strive to preserve the rich heritage of sound recording for future generations.
