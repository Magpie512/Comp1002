    About The Rohwedder Company

    The Man Behind the Innovation

    Otto Frederick Rohwedder (1880-1960) was an American inventor and engineer who revolutionized the baking industry with his groundbreaking bread-slicing machine.

    Early Life

    Born July 7, 1880, in Davenport, Iowa, Rohwedder studied engineering at the University of Iowa. After working as a jeweler and watchmaker, he turned his attention to solving one of household America's most pressing needs: convenient bread.

    The Journey to Innovation

    Beginning in 1912, Rohwedder dedicated himself to perfecting the bread-slicing machine. Despite a devastating fire in 1917 that destroyed his first prototype, his determination never wavered.

    A Historic Breakthrough

    On July 7, 1928, Rohwedder's 48th birthday, the first commercially sliced bread was sold in Chillicothe, Missouri. The response was immediate and overwhelming. Within months, bakeries nationwide adopted this revolutionary technology.

    Legacy

    Sliced bread became an American household staple, inspiring the famous phrase "the greatest thing since sliced bread." Otto Frederick Rohwedder's vision continues to simplify daily life for millions around the world.
